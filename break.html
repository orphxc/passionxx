The Breakfast Club

Director: John Hughes

"The Breakfast Club" is a beloved teen drama that follows five high school students from different social groups as they spend a Saturday in detention. Through their conversations and conflicts, they discover they have more in common than they ever expected.

“We're all pretty bizarre. Some of us are just better at hiding it, that's all.”

About the Director

John Hughes was an iconic American filmmaker known for defining teen cinema in the 1980s. Through films like "The Breakfast Club," "Ferris Bueller’s Day Off," and "Sixteen Candles," Hughes gave voice to adolescent struggles with humor, insight, and heart.
